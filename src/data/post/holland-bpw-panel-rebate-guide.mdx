Holland Board of Public Works (BPW) offers a **$1,000 rebate** for residential electric panel upgrades — one of the most generous local incentive programs in West Michigan. If you live in the Holland BPW service area and are planning a panel upgrade, an EV charger, or any kind of home electrification, this rebate can substantially offset the project cost. Here is exactly how it works, how to qualify, and what we handle on your behalf to make sure the paperwork lands cleanly.

## Quick Answer

- **Rebate amount:** $1,000 toward a residential panel upgrade.
- **Required minimum service:** 200 amps (upgrade from 100 or 150 amp to 200 amp qualifies).
- **Trigger requirement:** install an EV charger OR at least one 240V electric appliance (converted from natural gas) within 12 months of the panel upgrade.
- **Must be:** completed by a licensed electrician with a closed electrical permit.
- **Documentation needed:** permit copy plus proof of charger or appliance purchase and installation.
- **Pre-approval:** recommended — call BPW at 616-355-1577 before starting work.

## What the Rebate Covers

The Holland BPW rebate is structured to encourage two things at once: **electrification** (replacing gas appliances with electric, or adding an EV) **and the panel capacity to support it**. You can't just upgrade the panel and collect — you also have to electrify something. Conversely, you can't just install an EV charger on an old 100-amp panel and collect — the panel has to upgrade to 200 amps.

The combination is what BPW pays for, because the combination is what reduces grid load growth and supports their long-term electrification planning.

**Qualifying electrification installations include:**

- A Level 2 EV charger (NEMA 14-50 outlet or hardwired)
- An electric range or oven (replacing a gas range)
- An electric clothes dryer (replacing a gas dryer)
- A heat pump (replacing natural-gas heat)
- An electric water heater (replacing a gas water heater)
- An induction cooktop (replacing a gas cooktop)

**Important:** the electric appliance must be installed _within 12 months_ of the panel upgrade. You can do the panel first and the appliance later, but the clock starts ticking the day the panel passes inspection.

## Who Qualifies

- **Service territory:** You must be a Holland BPW electric customer. Check your utility bill — if BPW is your electric provider, you qualify. (Some Holland-area homes are served by Consumers Energy or other providers; they don't qualify for this specific program.)
- **Residential only:** This rebate is for single-family and small multi-family dwellings, not commercial.
- **Licensed electrician required:** The work has to be done by a licensed Michigan electrician with a closed electrical permit from the local AHJ.
- **Pre-approval recommended:** Call BPW at 616-355-1577 _before_ starting the project to confirm you qualify and to get the application started. This avoids surprises after the work is done.

## The Application Process (Step-by-Step)

1. **Pre-approval call.** Contact Holland BPW at **616-355-1577** before starting work. They confirm your service-area eligibility and walk you through the application requirements.
2. **Panel upgrade.** A licensed electrician (us, or another Michigan-licensed electrician of your choice) replaces your existing panel with a new 200-amp panel, pulls the electrical permit from the City of Holland, and schedules the final inspection.
3. **Electric appliance or EV charger install.** Within 12 months of the panel upgrade, install the qualifying electrification item. This can be at the same time as the panel upgrade (cleanest) or any time within the year.
4. **Documentation packet.** Submit to BPW: (a) the closed/passed electrical permit, (b) proof of purchase for the EV charger or electric appliance, (c) proof of installation, (d) the rebate application form (available from BPW).
5. **Approval and payment.** BPW reviews the packet and issues the $1,000 rebate after approval.

## Real-World Pairings That Work for the Rebate

The single most common project we run for the BPW rebate combines two things most Holland homeowners are already considering:

**Panel upgrade + Level 2 EV charger.** You get the modern electrical capacity you'd want anyway, the EV charger you're planning to install, and a $1,000 rebate. The Level 2 charger usually pays for the install cost on its own (most chargers fit comfortably in the rebate). See our [Level 1 vs Level 2 EV charger guide](/blog/level-1-vs-level-2-ev-charger) for charger selection details.

**Panel upgrade + heat pump.** Bigger project, but the rebate combined with federal IRA tax credits for heat pumps stacks to substantial savings. Heat pump replacing natural gas furnace and AC is the highest-impact electrification many homes can do.

**Panel upgrade + electric range / dryer.** Cleanest swap if you're already remodeling a kitchen or laundry room. Gas line stays in place (you can always switch back later if you want), the appliance change is straightforward, and the panel capacity supports the new electric loads cleanly.

## What Doesn't Count

- **Like-for-like panel replacement** (100-amp to 100-amp) — has to be at minimum 200 amp.
- **Subpanel additions** — the main service has to upgrade.
- **Electrification without panel upgrade** — adding an EV charger to an existing 200-amp panel doesn't qualify for this rebate (though it may qualify for separate Consumers Energy or DTE programs).
- **DIY work** — has to be a licensed electrician with a closed permit.
- **Commercial buildings** — residential only.

## Stack With Other Programs

The BPW rebate stacks cleanly with several other incentives:

- **Federal Section 30C tax credit** for EV chargers (30% up to $1,000) — separate from BPW, claim on your federal taxes for the year the charger is placed in service.
- **Federal IRA tax credits** for heat pumps and electrical panel upgrades (varies by income; see current IRS guidance).
- **Consumers Energy PowerMIDrive** — $500 cash back on a qualifying Level 2 EV charger install (up to $1,000 for income-qualified customers). Applies to Consumers Energy customers — different service territory than Holland BPW, so this stacks for nearby homes outside BPW limits, not for BPW customers themselves.
- **DTE Energy charger rebates** — similar program for DTE customers.
- **Manufacturer rebates** on specific EV chargers, heat pumps, or appliances — vendor-by-vendor.

Total potential savings stacking BPW + federal credit + manufacturer rebate on a typical panel-upgrade + EV-charger project can exceed $2,000 for Holland BPW customers. Consumers Energy customers in the surrounding area can stack PowerMIDrive + federal credit + manufacturer rebate for similar combined savings on the EV-charger side, though without the BPW $1,000 panel piece.

## What If I'm Outside Holland BPW Territory?

If you're in Grand Rapids, Hudsonville, Zeeland, Allegan, Ada, or another area served by Consumers Energy or DTE, you don't qualify for the BPW rebate specifically — but those utilities run their own programs. **Consumers Energy's PowerMIDrive** pays $500 cash back for qualifying Level 2 charger installs (up to $1,000 for income-qualified customers). DTE Energy runs a similar program for its service area. ## FAQs

**How long does the BPW rebate approval take?**
Typically 4-8 weeks after BPW receives the completed application packet. Submitting clean documentation (permit closed, proof of purchase, proof of installation, application form) speeds it up. Missing or incomplete documentation is the #1 cause of delays.

**Can I get the rebate if I install the EV charger first and the panel later?**
No. The panel upgrade is the trigger; the electrification has to follow (or happen simultaneously). The 12-month clock starts from the panel inspection date.

**Does the rebate cover the cost of the EV charger or appliance itself?**
No. The $1,000 is specifically for the panel upgrade scope. The EV charger or appliance is the _qualifying condition_, not what the rebate pays for. Other programs (federal credits, manufacturer rebates) can offset the appliance side.

**What if my panel upgrade is more expensive than expected?**
The rebate is a fixed $1,000 regardless of project cost. We give you a written estimate before any work starts so the math is clear from the beginning.

**Is the BPW rebate guaranteed?**
The program is current as of this writing but BPW can change or end it at any time. Pre-approval (the 616-355-1577 call before starting work) is the only way to lock in your eligibility before committing to the project.
