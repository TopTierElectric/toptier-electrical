A commercial LED retrofit is the rare capital project with predictable returns: cut lighting energy 50–75%, cut maintenance 60–80%, and most West Michigan businesses see payback in 18–36 months. The math is straightforward once you have your current fixture count, run hours, and utility rate. Here is how to calculate ROI for your specific facility and the rebates that shorten the payback further.

## Quick Answer

- **Typical energy savings:** 50–75% on lighting load.
- **Typical payback:** 18–36 months for retail/office; 12–24 months for warehouse/industrial.
- **Maintenance savings:** 5–10× longer lamp life vs T8 fluorescent.
- **Consumers Energy / DTE rebates:** $0.05–$0.25 per kWh saved annually.
- **Federal 179D deduction:** up to $0.60/sq ft for qualifying buildings.

## The ROI Formula

```
Annual savings = (old wattage − new wattage) × run hours × kWh rate
Payback = (project cost − rebates) ÷ annual savings
```

Two inputs you need:

1. **Current state:** total connected lighting load in watts × annual run hours.
2. **Retrofit state:** new LED wattage × same run hours, plus install cost.

### Example: Mid-sized retail (5,000 sq ft)

- Current: 80 troffers × 64 W (T8 fluorescent + ballast) = 5,120 W
- Annual run hours: 4,000 (12 hrs/day, 333 days/year)
- Current annual energy: 20,480 kWh
- At $0.12/kWh: **$2,458/year on lighting**

After LED retrofit:
- 80 LED troffer replacements × 32 W = 2,560 W
- Annual energy: 10,240 kWh
- New cost: **$1,229/year**
- **Savings: $1,229/year (50%)**

Project cost: $80/fixture × 80 = $6,400 installed
Less rebates: −$2,000 (Consumers Energy at $0.10/kWh saved × 10,240)
Net cost: $4,400

**Payback: $4,400 ÷ $1,229 = 3.6 years**

That's the conservative case. Warehouse and 24/7 industrial installs hit 12–18 month paybacks because run hours are higher and starting wattage (400W metal halide) is much higher.

## Where the Savings Actually Come From

Three components, in order of magnitude:

### 1. Lower wattage at the fixture (50–75% reduction)
A 32W LED troffer replaces a 64W T8 troffer (ballast + lamps). A 100W LED high-bay replaces a 400W metal halide. The wattage difference is the most direct savings.

### 2. Reduced HVAC load
LED fixtures emit far less waste heat. A 5,000 sq ft retail space converting 5 kW of fluorescent to 2.5 kW of LED also cuts ~1.5 tons of cooling load in summer. Annual HVAC savings often add 10–15% on top of direct lighting savings.

### 3. Reduced maintenance
Fluorescent tubes last 20,000–30,000 hours; LED fixtures last 50,000–100,000 hours. Plus, ballasts (the failure-prone part of fluorescent systems) are eliminated. A retail facility that replaces 8–10 ballasts and 20–30 tubes per year saves $1,500–$3,500 annually in maintenance time and material.

## Utility Rebates in West Michigan

**Consumers Energy** Business Lighting program:
- $0.05–$0.25 per kWh saved annually (varies by fixture type)
- Pre-approval required for projects >$5,000
- Custom rebates for unusual retrofits
- Self-direct option for very large customers

**DTE Energy** Business Solutions:
- Similar per-kWh structure
- Bonus tiers for small business and food service
- Streamlined approval for standard fixture swaps

**Holland Board of Public Works** and other munis:
- Varies; typically smaller programs but often easier paperwork
- Check directly with your utility

In every commercial retrofit we quote, rebates are factored into the net cost before payback math. Most projects net 25–40% rebate against gross cost.

## Federal Tax Treatment

Beyond rebates, two federal mechanisms apply:

**Section 179 deduction:** retrofits typically qualify as immediate expense rather than capital depreciation. Talk to your CPA — for many businesses this is the biggest financial lever.

**Section 179D Energy-Efficient Commercial Buildings Deduction:** up to $0.60/sq ft for qualifying envelope/lighting/HVAC efficiency improvements that exceed ASHRAE baselines. More relevant for whole-building retrofits than lighting-only.

## When to Retrofit vs Replace Fixtures

Two paths to the same outcome:

**Retrofit (lamp + driver kit in existing fixture):**
- Cheaper: $40–$100/fixture installed.
- Faster: 5–10 fixtures per electrician-hour.
- Reuses existing housing.
- Best for fixtures that are still cosmetically and electrically sound.

**Full fixture replacement:**
- More expensive: $80–$250/fixture installed.
- Slower: 2–4 fixtures per electrician-hour.
- New, longer-warranty housing and driver.
- Best when existing fixtures are damaged, undersized, or aesthetically dated.

We typically recommend retrofit for offices with newer T8 troffers (post-2000) and full replacement for older warehouses with metal halide high-bays. ## What Slows Payback

- **Low run hours.** A space lit 8 hrs/day, 5 days/week (2,000 annual hours) has half the savings of a 4,000-hour space.
- **Already-LED facilities.** Replacing first-gen LEDs with current-gen yields smaller wattage drops.
- **Spaces with daylighting credit.** Retrofits in skylight-rich zones see less load shift.
- **Off-peak electric rates.** TOU customers on low overnight rates have lower per-kWh savings.

## What Speeds Payback

- **24/7 operation.** Warehouses, distribution, manufacturing.
- **Old metal halide or high-pressure sodium.** Wattage drop is dramatic.
- **High utility rates.** Industrial customers paying $0.14+/kWh see fastest returns.
- **Bundled with controls (occupancy sensors, daylight harvesting).** Adds 10–25% additional savings on top.

## FAQs

**How long does a commercial LED retrofit take?**
A 50–100 fixture retrofit typically completes in 1–3 nights of off-hours work to minimize business disruption. Larger facilities phase the work over a week or two.

**Do I have to shut down for the retrofit?**
No. We schedule fixture swaps after-hours or on a rolling per-zone basis so business operations continue. Power is only off in the immediate work area.

**Are the rebates worth the paperwork?**
For projects >$3,000, yes — almost always. The utility program manager handles most documentation; we provide what they need. Smaller projects sometimes skip the rebate for time, but it costs you 20–30% of net savings.

**What if my fixtures are mixed (some new, some old)?**
We assess each fixture type separately and recommend retrofit or replacement per zone. Phased approaches (oldest first) are common.

**Does the retrofit require an electrical permit?**
Like-for-like fixture swaps usually don't require a permit. New circuits or major rewiring does. We handle the determination during the site walk.
