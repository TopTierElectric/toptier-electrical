Michigan EV charger rebates can knock $300–$1,000 off the installed cost of a Level 2 home charger — if you know which program fits your utility, vehicle, and income. The two big residential programs come from Consumers Energy and DTE, with a smaller charger rebate from municipal utilities like Holland Board of Public Works. One important 2026 change up front: the federal 30C tax credit that used to stack on top of these expired June 30, 2026 (details below). Here is the current landscape, what each pays, and what to verify before you sign anything.

## Quick Answer

- **Consumers Energy PowerMIDrive:** **$500 rebate** (up to **$1,000** income-qualified) + reduced overnight rates.
- **DTE Home EV Charger Rebate:** **$500 rebate** for income-qualified customers + EV time-of-use plan.
- **Holland BPW:** **$300 charger rebate** ($325 for an ENERGY STAR unit), plus a separate **$1,000 panel-upgrade rebate**.
- **Federal credit (30C):** **expired June 30, 2026** — no longer available for new installs.
- Stack a utility charger rebate with a panel-upgrade rebate where you qualify.

## Consumers Energy: PowerMIDrive

PowerMIDrive is Consumers Energy's residential EV program. It includes:

- **$500 charger rebate** for installing a qualified networked Level 2 charger.
- **Up to $1,000 charger rebate** for income-qualified customers (verified through Consumers Energy).
- **Overnight rate** (typically 11 PM – 7 AM) at roughly half the standard rate.
- **Whole-home time-of-use plan** as an alternative for households that can shift load.
- **$20 Amazon gift card** refer-a-friend bonus (both the referrer and the new participant receive the card after the friend joins the program).

Eligibility requirements:

- Residential Consumers Energy customer.
- Charger from the program's approved hardware list (networked / Wi-Fi-enabled).
- Installed by a licensed electrician with permit and inspection records.
- Sign up for an EV rate plan within 90 days of charger activation.

The approved hardware list rotates — confirm with Consumers Energy before purchase. Many ChargePoint, Wallbox, Enel X, and Emporia units qualify.

## DTE: Home EV Charger Rebate

DTE Energy's residential program (part of DTE's broader "Charging Forward" EV effort) works much like PowerMIDrive, but it is income-qualified:

- **$500 rebate** toward a qualifying Level 2 charger for income-eligible customers (household income under DTE's published threshold for your household size).
- **EV time-of-use plan** with discounted overnight rates.
- Charger must be **ENERGY STAR certified or the same manufacturer as your EV**, and you must own or lease an EV.

Requirements otherwise mirror Consumers: licensed electrician, permit, and EV-rate enrollment. Because DTE's amount and income thresholds are updated periodically, confirm the current figures on DTE's Home EV Charger Rebate page before you buy.

## Federal Tax Credit (30C) — Expired June 30, 2026

Worth stating plainly, because a lot of older guides (and some installers) still quote it: the federal Section 30C EV-charger tax credit — 30% of equipment and installation, up to $1,000 for a home charger — **expired for equipment placed in service after June 30, 2026.** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed in July 2025, ended it early; the Inflation Reduction Act's old 2032 sunset no longer applies.

If your charger was placed in service on or before June 30, 2026, you may still be able to claim it on **IRS Form 8911** for that tax year — check with your tax advisor. For any install after that date, plan your numbers around the utility rebates above, not the federal credit.

## Holland Board of Public Works & Other Munis

Holland BPW does run residential EV incentives through its Charge Up Holland program: a **$300 rebate on a wall-mounted Level 2 charger ($325 if the unit is ENERGY STAR certified)**, with enrollment in BPW's time-of-use rate. Separately, BPW offers a **$1,000 rebate for upgrading to a 200-amp panel** when you add an EV charger or a 240V gas-to-electric appliance within 12 months — so a Holland customer whose charger install also needs a panel upgrade can claim both. ## What You Need to Document

To claim either utility rebate plus the federal credit, save:

1. **Itemized invoice** showing equipment + labor breakdown.
2. **Charger serial number** and model.
3. **Permit** and **passing inspection** documentation.
4. **Proof of licensed electrician** (license number on the invoice).
5. **Charger network registration** (proof the charger is online and reporting to the program).
6. **Census tract lookup** (federal credit eligibility).

## Common Disqualifications

- **Non-networked chargers.** A Wi-Fi-disabled hardwired Tesla Wall Connector or generic dumb charger usually does not qualify for utility rebates (federal credit may still apply).
- **Unpermitted installs.** No permit, no rebate, no exceptions.
- **Self-installs.** Both utilities require a licensed electrician on the install invoice.
- **Late enrollment.** Sign up for the EV rate plan within the program's stated window (usually 90 days after charger activation).

## Timing Your Install for Maximum Rebate

If you're flexible on timing, two windows tend to be best:

- **Spring (March–May):** utility funding budgets reset; quickest rebate processing.
- **Fall (Sept–Oct):** budget remains, plus dealer EV incentives often align.

Year-end installs are fine but can face slower rebate processing through the holiday window.

## FAQs

**Is the federal EV-charger tax credit still available?**
No — the federal 30C credit expired for chargers placed in service after June 30, 2026. If yours went in on or before that date, ask your tax advisor about claiming it on IRS Form 8911 for that tax year. For installs after that, plan around the utility rebates instead.

**Do used or refurbished chargers qualify?**
Utility rebates: usually no — they require new networked (or ENERGY STAR) hardware from the approved list. Buy the charger new and on-list to stay eligible.

**What if my panel needs upgrading before the install?**
That's common when adding a 48A charger to an older 100-amp service. Some utilities rebate the panel upgrade itself — Holland BPW pays $1,000 toward a 200-amp upgrade when it's paired with an EV charger. We'll tell you at the site visit whether your panel has capacity or needs upsizing.

**Are commercial chargers eligible for these residential rebates?**
No — commercial installs have separate programs (PowerMIDrive Business, DTE Charging Forward Workplace). The federal 30C credit has a separate commercial calculation.

**Do utility rebates change year to year?**
Yes. Funding levels, eligible hardware, and enrollment windows shift annually. Confirm current terms before you commit.
